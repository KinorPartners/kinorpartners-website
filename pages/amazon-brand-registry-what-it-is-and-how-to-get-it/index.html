Amazon Brand Registry: What It Is and How to Get It

Amazon Brand Registry is one of the most valuable programs available to Amazon sellers. It unlocks powerful tools that protect your brand, improve your listings, and give you a competitive edge. If you sell branded products on Amazon, Brand Registry is not optional - it is essential.

What Is Amazon Brand Registry?

Amazon Brand Registry is a free program that gives brand owners access to a suite of tools designed to protect intellectual property, enhance product listings, and improve the overall shopping experience for customers. Once enrolled, your brand is formally recognized by Amazon, giving you more control over your product pages and access to exclusive marketing tools.

Key Benefits of Amazon Brand Registry

1. A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content)

A+ Content allows you to replace the standard product description with rich media modules - comparison charts, lifestyle images, feature callout sections, and more. Research consistently shows A+ Content improves conversion rates by 5-10%, and Premium A+ Content (available to qualifying brands) can improve conversion by up to 20%.

2. Amazon Storefront

Brand Registry lets you build a free, multi-page branded storefront on Amazon with your own URL (amazon.com/stores/YourBrand). This is your dedicated space to tell your brand story, showcase your full product catalog, and capture customers who search for your brand directly.

3. Brand Analytics

Brand Analytics gives you access to powerful search data that non-registered sellers cannot see. This includes the Amazon Search Terms Report (showing what shoppers search before buying your products), Market Basket Analysis (showing what products customers buy alongside yours), and Item Comparison data.

4. Sponsored Brand Ads

Only Brand Registry members can run Sponsored Brand campaigns - the headline ads that appear at the top of Amazon search results with your logo, a custom headline, and multiple products. These ads are excellent for brand awareness and driving traffic to your Amazon Store.

5. Brand Protection Tools

Brand Registry gives you automated protections against trademark violations, counterfeit products, and unauthorized listing changes. You can submit infringement reports directly, and Amazon proactively monitors for potential violations using your brand name and product images.

6. Vine Program

Brand Registry members can enroll new products in the Amazon Vine program, which allows Amazon's top trusted reviewers to receive your product for free in exchange for honest reviews. This helps you accumulate early reviews during your product launch window.

How to Qualify for Amazon Brand Registry

To enroll in Brand Registry, you need an active registered trademark in the country where you want to enroll. The trademark must be a text-based mark or an image-based mark containing words, letters, or numbers.

Step 1: Apply for a Trademark

In the United States, file your trademark application with the USPTO (United States Patent and Trademark Office). The application process takes approximately 8-12 months for standard examination. The cost is $250-$350 per class of goods. File under Class 35 (retail services) or the specific class that applies to your product category.

Step 2: Apply via IP Accelerator

Amazon's IP Accelerator program connects sellers with pre-vetted trademark attorneys who can file your application. Importantly, IP Accelerator approval can grant you temporary Brand Registry access while your trademark application is pending - dramatically reducing your waiting time.

Step 3: Enroll in Brand Registry

Once your trademark is registered (or you have IP Accelerator approval), go to brandregistry.amazon.com and submit your enrollment application. You will need your trademark registration number, a list of product categories, and images of your branded products or packaging.

Brand Registry vs No Brand Registry: The Real Cost

Sellers without Brand Registry are significantly disadvantaged. They cannot suppress unauthorized listing changes from other sellers, cannot add A+ Content, cannot run Sponsored Brand ads, and are more vulnerable to counterfeiters. For any seller doing more than $3,000-$5,000 per month, the investment in Brand Registry (primarily the trademark cost) pays for itself quickly through improved conversion rates alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Brand Registry take to get approved?

Once you have a registered trademark, Brand Registry enrollment typically takes 2-10 business days for Amazon to review and approve. Via IP Accelerator with a pending trademark application, provisional access can be granted within days of your trademark filing.

Does Brand Registry cost money?

Brand Registry itself is free. The cost is in obtaining the trademark: USPTO trademark fees are $250-$350 per class, plus attorney fees if you use one ($500-$2,000 typically). Total out-of-pocket is typically $800-$2,500 for a basic trademark application.

Can I use Brand Registry for multiple products?

Yes. Brand Registry covers all products sold under your registered trademark. If you sell multiple product lines under one brand, all products benefit from Brand Registry once your brand is enrolled.

What happens to my Brand Registry if my trademark expires?

Amazon may revoke your Brand Registry access if your trademark registration lapses. US trademarks must be renewed between the 5th and 6th year after registration, and again every 10 years. Mark these renewal dates and treat trademark maintenance as a business priority.
