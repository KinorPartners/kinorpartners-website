How to Launch a Product on Amazon: Step-by-Step Guide

A successful Amazon product launch is a carefully orchestrated event - not a moment where you list a product and hope for the best. The first 30-60 days set the trajectory for your entire product lifespan. This guide walks you through every step.

Why the Amazon Launch Phase Is So Critical

Amazon rewards new products with a brief period of elevated visibility sometimes called the honeymoon period. During this window, Amazon gives new listings extra impressions to gauge how buyers respond. If your conversion rate is strong during this period, you earn sustained organic ranking. If it is weak, the algorithm deprioritizes your listing and recovery becomes far more difficult.

This means your launch must be built around one objective: maximizing sales velocity and conversion rate in the first 30 days.

Pre-Launch: The Foundation Work

A product launch starts weeks before your listing goes live. The quality of your preparation determines the outcome.

1. Validate Your Product Before Launch

Use Amazon Best Seller Rank (BSR) data, review analysis tools like Helium 10 Review Insights, and keyword volume data to confirm genuine market demand before spending on inventory. Analyze the top 3 competitors: their review counts, their pricing, and the gaps in their listings that you can exploit.

2. Build a Fully Optimized Listing Before Day 1

Your listing must be complete and compelling before you send a single unit of traffic to it. This means professional photography (hero shot, lifestyle images, infographic images), a keyword-optimized title, five benefit-led bullet points, A+ Content if you have Brand Registry, and backend search terms filled to the 250-byte limit.

3. Set Up Your PPC Campaigns in Advance

Have your Sponsored Products auto campaigns ready to launch on day one. Set initial bids at 10-20% above the suggested bid to ensure strong impressions during the critical early window. Allocate a launch budget - typically 3-5x your steady-state monthly budget - for the first 60 days.

4. Prepare Your Inventory

Nothing kills a launch faster than going out of stock during peak velocity. Use conservative sales projections and add a 30% buffer. For FBA, ensure your shipment arrives at the fulfillment center at least 2 weeks before launch day.

Launch Day: Activating the Flywheel

5. Go Live and Activate PPC Immediately

The moment your listing is live and in stock, activate your PPC campaigns. Do not wait. Every day without advertising during the honeymoon period is a missed opportunity. Run Sponsored Products auto campaigns plus 2-3 manual campaigns targeting your top priority keywords.

6. Drive External Traffic

Use Amazon Attribution links to drive traffic from external sources - email lists, social media, influencer partnerships, and Google Shopping ads. Each external conversion signals Amazon that your product is in high demand, amplifying your organic ranking boost.

7. Activate Your Launch Discount or Coupon

A 15-25% launch discount or a prominent on-page coupon lowers the psychological barrier for first buyers and directly improves conversion rate. Amazon surfaces coupon listings more prominently in search results, giving you extra visibility.

Post-Launch: Sustaining Momentum (Days 30-90)

8. Request Reviews the Right Way

Use the Request a Review button in Seller Central for every order. This sends Amazon official review request emails. Do not incentivize reviews or use third-party review services - both violate Amazon policy and risk account suspension.

9. Monitor and Optimize Weekly

Week-by-week tracking disciplines: check your BSR trajectory, organic rank for your 3-5 primary keywords, PPC ACoS, and conversion rate. Make one or two targeted optimizations at a time so you can isolate what is working.

10. Transition from Launch to Profitability Mode

After 60-90 days with 15+ reviews and stable organic rank, gradually reduce launch discounts and transition PPC from high-volume harvesting to profitability optimization. Tighten bids, pause non-performers, and scale proven winners.

Common Launch Mistakes

  • Launching with a listing that is not fully optimized - always complete the listing first
  • Going out of stock in the first 90 days - this resets your ranking and is very difficult to recover from
  • Setting PPC budgets too low during the launch phase
  • Not responding to early negative reviews immediately - one or two three-star reviews in the first week can permanently suppress your launch
  • Pricing too high relative to competitors during launch phase

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an Amazon product launch take?

The active launch phase is typically 60-90 days. Within that window, you can establish a stable BSR, accumulate reviews, and achieve page-one organic ranking for your primary keywords if the fundamentals are strong.

How much inventory should I order for my first Amazon launch?

A common formula: project units per day based on competitor BSR benchmarks, multiply by 60 days, and add a 30% buffer. For a product projecting 5 units per day, order 5 x 60 x 1.3 = 390 units minimum.

Do I need Brand Registry to launch on Amazon?

No, but Brand Registry is strongly recommended. It unlocks A+ Content, Brand Stores, and Sponsored Brand ads - all proven to improve launch conversion rates. Apply for Brand Registry as early as your trademark allows.

What is the most important metric during an Amazon launch?

Conversion rate (CVR). During the honeymoon period, Amazon is measuring how well your listing converts impressions into sales. High CVR earns sustained ranking; low CVR squanders the opportunity. Everything - photography, copy, price, reviews - is in service of conversion rate.
