Frequently Asked Questions
Quick answers to the most common questions Amazon sellers ask us
What is the most important factor for ranking on Amazon?
Sales velocity and conversion rate are the two biggest drivers of Amazon rankings. Amazon's A9 algorithm prioritizes products that generate the most revenue per customer search. That means your listing needs to convert well (strong images, compelling title, clear bullet points) and you need consistent sales momentum — ideally supported by PPC advertising in the early stages. Keyword relevance is the foundation, but conversion and sales velocity are what sustain rankings over time.
How long does it take to see results on Amazon?
Most new sellers see meaningful traction within 60–90 days when following a structured launch strategy. Initial keyword rankings typically improve within 2–4 weeks if you're running targeted PPC and your listing is properly optimized. Review accumulation usually begins in weeks 3–6. Sustainable organic ranking and profitability typically emerge in months 3–6. Brands working with an experienced Amazon agency often reach these milestones faster by eliminating common mistakes.
Should I use FBA or FBM for my products?
FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) is the right choice for most small businesses selling products under 10 lbs with healthy margins, because it gives you Prime eligibility and handles all logistics. FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) makes more sense for oversized, heavy, or fragile items where FBA fees would destroy margins. Many successful sellers use a hybrid approach — FBA for fast-moving SKUs and FBM as a backup.
How much should I spend on Amazon PPC?
A common starting benchmark is spending 10–15% of your target monthly revenue on PPC during the launch phase, then scaling back as organic rankings improve. Focus on ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale) — most sellers target 20–35% ACOS depending on their margins. The key is reinvesting in profitable campaigns and cutting spend on underperformers.
Is Amazon Brand Registry worth it for small businesses?
Absolutely. Brand Registry is one of the highest-leverage moves a small business can make on Amazon. It unlocks A+ Content (which can increase conversion rates by 3–10%), Sponsored Brand ads, the Brand Store, and advanced anti-counterfeiting tools. The only requirement is a registered trademark.
