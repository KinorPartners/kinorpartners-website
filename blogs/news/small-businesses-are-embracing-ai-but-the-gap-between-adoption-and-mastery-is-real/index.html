Small Businesses Are Embracing AI - But the Gap Between Adoption and M

Executive Summary

A March 2026 Goldman Sachs survey of over 1,200 small business owners reveals a striking paradox: AI adoption is booming, but true integration is rare. 76% of small businesses are already using AI, and 93% report positive results — yet only 14% have embedded AI fully into their core operations. The #1 barrier? A lack of training and support. This is precisely why Kinor Partners exists: to bridge that gap and turn early AI adoption into a real competitive advantage.

The Big Picture: AI Is Here — But Most Small Businesses Are Just Getting Started

Goldman Sachs released new research in March 2026 — based on their 10,000 Small Businesses Voices survey conducted by Babson College and David Binder Research — and the findings are both encouraging and sobering. Small businesses across all 50 states are embracing AI at remarkable speed. But enthusiasm alone isn't enough. The gap between "using AI" and "mastering AI" is wide — and it's costing small businesses real growth.

At Kinor Partners, we work every day to help small businesses close exactly that gap. These findings validate what we see on the ground: the opportunity is enormous, and the need for expert guidance has never been greater.

Our Commentary

The Goldman Sachs data tells a story we recognize intimately. Small business owners are resourceful and fast-moving — they are absolutely embracing AI tools. But there is a meaningful difference between experimenting with AI and building a systematic, automated engine that drives consistent revenue growth.

The 14% who have fully integrated AI are not necessarily smarter or better-funded than the other 86%. They simply had a roadmap, the right tools, and — critically — the right partners to help them implement. That is the gap Kinor Partners was built to fill, specifically for marketing, sales, and eCommerce channels like Amazon.

The survey also highlights a telling finding: 87% of small business owners see AI as a tool to augment their workforce, not replace it. We couldn't agree more. Our philosophy is centered on deploying AI to amplify your team's capabilities — automating the repetitive, high-volume tasks so your people can focus on relationship-building, strategy, and closing.

Key Takeaways from the Goldman Sachs Survey

  • 76% of small businesses are currently using AI — adoption is mainstream, not emerging.
  • 93% report a positive business impact — AI works. The skeptics are being left behind.
  • 84% cite increased efficiency and productivity as the top benefit.
  • 67% expect AI to increase revenue — small businesses are betting on AI for growth.
  • 87% say AI augments rather than replaces employees — AI is a teammate, not a threat.
  • Only 14% have fully integrated AI into core operations — the majority are stuck in early-stage adoption.
  • 73% say they need more training and implementation resources — the demand for expert guidance is real and urgent.
  • Key barriers: lack of technical expertise, difficulty choosing the right tools, and data privacy concerns.
