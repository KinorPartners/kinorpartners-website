The hardest step is from 'Later' to Launch: Why Your Amazon Strategy C

It is hard to start. Everyone is on Amazon, but not you. Higher priorities always grabs your attention - operation challenges, urgent customer requests, tariff, and many other business needs.

It is time though to take the first step.

But for the busy, growing business, taking that step often feels like taking three steps backward. You know the potential of Amazon is massive, yet the internal bandwidth to tackle a new channel—one that requires deep expertise in SEO, advertising, inventory, and compliance—simply doesn't exist.

The True Cost of Delay and DIY

The default solutions to this problem are almost always frustrating:

  • Hiring Someone is a Hassle (and Expensive): You could hire a dedicated in-house Amazon manager, but it is months before you see results. It is also very expensive. You have to spend time recruiting, onboarding, training, and hoping they have the right mix of operational, creative, and analytical skills. The ramp-up time alone often costs a full quarter of missed sales.

  • The Agency Roulette: Or, you search for a partner. Finding an agency is not easy either, I went through four before finding the right one that delivered on its promises. This isn't just a matter of wasted fees; it's a profound distraction. Low-quality agencies treat your account like a side project, relying on canned strategies and generic reports. They overcomplicate the simple and underdeliver on the essential.

The simple truth is: The work is not rocket science, but it takes significant effort to create a channel and navigate the complexity of the Amazon marketplace platform. It requires focused time, dedicated expertise, and a proven playbook.
